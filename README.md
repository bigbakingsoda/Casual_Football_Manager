# Casual_Football_Manager
Casual FM: The Ultimate Browser-Based Football Management Simulation Welcome to the big leagues. Casual FM isn't just about picking a team; it’s about building a legacy, managing a tactical "shape," and surviving the cutthroat world of promotion and relegation. Getting Started: Choose Your Path:Standard Leagues: Classic 20-team divisions across Europe. Super League: The world’s elite clubs condensed into a 4-tier pyramid. Create-A-Club: The beta mode. Build a club from scratch with a £500M budget. The Draft (for create-a-club only): You have £500M to sign a squad of up to 25 players. High-CA (Current Ability) players cost significantly more.The Identity: Set your primary/secondary colors and choose a Club Vision. This vision influences how you should build your squad (e.g., focusing on youth vs. high-rep stars).The Takeover: You must select an existing club to replace in the league hierarchy. Choose wisely—replacing a top-tier team means higher expectations! Tactical Mastery: The tactics screen is the heart of the engine. Unlike basic sims, player performance is tied to Positional Familiarity. Positional Familiarity Performance Multiplier🟢Natural 100% Efficiency🟡Accomplished 75% Efficiency🟠Competent 50% Efficiency🔴Awkward 5% Efficiency (Massive Star Rating Penalty) The "Drag & Drop" PitchCustom Shapes: You can drag player nodes anywhere on the pitch. The game will automatically update their role (e.g., dragging a DC slightly wider and higher might turn them into a WBL). Star Ratings: Stars are relative. A 5-star rating means the player is performing at their absolute peak in that specific spot. If you move a world-class Striker (ST) to Center Back (DC), their star rating will plummet because they don't know the role. The Living World: The simulation doesn't stop when the whistle blows.Youth Development: Every 4 weeks, players under 24 have a chance to increase their CA based on their PA (Potential Ability). Aging & Decay: Once players hit 31 (or 33 for Goalkeepers), their physical stats begin to decline. Keep an eye on your veterans—they may need replacing sooner than you think. Promotion & Relegation: At the end of 38 weeks, the bottom 3 teams are relegated to the "Lower Clubs" database, and the top 3 AI teams from the lower tier are promoted. Warning: If you are relegated, the board will sack you immediately, and you’ll have to find a new job in the Job Center. Pro Tips for Success: Balance the Budget: In Create-A-Club, don't spend all £500M on five superstars. You need a squad of 25 to survive injuries and fatigue throughout a 38-week season. Versatility is King: Look for players with multiple "Accomplished" positions. They are invaluable for covering injuries without losing tactical efficiency. The Job Hop: Don't be afraid to check the Job Center. If a bigger club with a massive transfer budget loses its manager, you can jump ship and take your tactical secrets with you. Watch the "CA": A player with 150 CA at age 32 is often worse than a 140 CA player at age 21 because the younger player will improve every month. Now, go out there and bring home the trophy!
